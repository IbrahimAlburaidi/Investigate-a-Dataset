Introduction¶

No-show Appointments dataset has been selected. It consists of 14 different columns showing the details of each appointment. Appointment ID, gender of the patient, scheduled day, appointment day, age of the patient, neighbourhood, having a scholarship or not, holding Hipertension or not, holding Diabetes or not, addicted to Alcohol or not, having a handcap or not, received a SMS message of the appointment or not, apear on the appointment or not are shown for every patient's appointment. This project will analyze the appointments and looking for the reasons behind the patient not showing on his/her appointment. Do the gender, age, scholarship, Hipertension, Diabetes, Alcoholism, handcap, receiving SMS message affect the patient to apear or his/her appointment or not?. These questions will be answered at the end of the project on the conclusion of the report.

Conclusions¶

To sum up, no-show appointments data has been analyzed to find out the factors affect the patient not showing up on his/her appointment. Six different factors have been checked which are: gender, age, holding Hipertension, holding Diabetes, Alcoholism, having handcap, receiving SMS message, having a scholarship. By using histogram, bar, and value count plots, it can be said that the gender, age, Diabetes, and scholarship are not affecting the patient appearance on his/her appointment while receiving SMS message by the appointment details affect the patient appearance since about 85% of those did not receive a message by their appointments showed up while only 70% of those received a message by their appointments showed up on. There are some factors which migh be considered as affecting and might not like: Holding Hipertension, addicting to Alcohol, and having a handcap.

Limitations: 1 More details are required for more precise and accurate analysis. 2 Scheduled and appointment dates are not clear enough to be analyzed. 3 Scholarship needs to be more specified by mentioning the level of scholarship. 4 Level of Hipertension, Diabetes, and Alcoholism need to be mentioned to have better analysis.
